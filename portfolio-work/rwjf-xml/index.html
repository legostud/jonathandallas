Comparing Health Care Quality: A National Directory

February 3, 2011
[Share this component]
  • Print
  • Email

Measuring and publicly reporting on the care doctors and hospitals provide are crucial to improving quality and lowering the cost of health care nationwide.

This interactive directory of 207 state and local public reports is intended to help patients find reliable information on the care in their communities. Enter your state to find public resources to help you choose a provider.

Disclaimer

Disclaimer: Reports included in this directory meet the following criteria: use performance measurement data based on nationally recognized measurement sets for quality of care in inpatient or outpatient settings; include measures for process of care, outcome of care, cost, efficiency, patient safety, utilization or patient experience; make reports publicly available without fee or subscription; and include comparative information for facilities or providers in a defined location, with the exception of the national resources. Please note that in some cases reports cover multiple states. For example, a report from a New York-based organization may be applicable to the state of New Hampshire.

RWJF compiled this online repository but makes no claims about the accuracy or usability of the information. RWJF recognizes that not all reports included in the directory are consumer friendly, as certain reports are targeted to industry professionals. Questions about any information in any specific report should be directed to the organizations that developed the report, listed on each report website.
